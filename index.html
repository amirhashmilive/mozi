The Doctrinal Foundation

Al-Mu'dhiyāt & Fawāsiq

In Islamic jurisprudence, creatures that pose actual harm (*dharar*) to human life, health, property, or food security are classified under *Al-Mu'dhiyāt*. The Prophet ﷺ explicitly permitted the removal of five (or six) harmful transgressive creatures, known as *Fawāsiq*, even within sacred states.

The Prophetic Command:

"Khamsun mina'd-dawabbi kulluhunna fasiqun yuqtalna fi'l-harami..."

"Five animals are harmful (fāsiq) and may be killed even in the Haram: the crow, the kite, the scorpion, the rat, and the biting dog." (Sahih al-Bukhari 1829)

The Scientific Integration

Integrated Pest Management

Modern epidemiology and entomology validate these rulings. The creatures identified in scripture are primary vectors of lethal zoonotic pathogens (Rabies, Leptospirosis, Plague) or possess dangerous venom.

Our platform merges these traditions, advocating for structural exclusion, sanitation, and humane targeted elimination instead of indiscriminate mass extermination.
