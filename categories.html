Creature Classifications

Islamic jurisprudence and modern biosecurity divide creatures into five distinct operational categories.

1. Venomous Creatures (Extreme Risk)

Scorpions, snakes, wasps, and centipedes. Rulings permit their elimination when they pose an immediate threat. Direct physical harm triggers the permissibility of culling to protect life.

Explore Venomous Creatures

2. Disease Vectors (High Risk)

Mosquitoes, flies, bed bugs, cockroaches, and ticks. As mechanical or biological carriers of deadly pathogens, their control falls under the Islamic mandate of sanitation and biosecurity.

Explore Disease Vectors

3. Protected Species (Absolute Prohibitions)

Honeybees, ants, frogs, and hoopoe birds. The Prophet ﷺ explicitly forbade killing these creatures. They must be relocated safely and preserved for ecological balance (*mizan*).
