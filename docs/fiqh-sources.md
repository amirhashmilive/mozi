# MOZI — Classical Fiqh Sources

This document lists the authoritative classical texts of Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) and Hadith commentaries (*shuruh*) used to compile the rulings for Al-Mu'dhiyat on the MOZI platform.

---

## 1. Hadith Commentaries (Shurūh)

*   **Fath al-Bārī bi-Sharh Sahīh al-Bukhārī** — *Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani* (Shafi'i school)
    *   *Application:* Primary explanation of the Fawasiq Hadith, discussions on what constitutes a "transgressor" (*fasiq*), and boundaries of killing.
*   **Al-Minhāj bi-Sharh Sahīh Muslim ibn al-Hajjāj** — *Imam al-Nawawi* (Shafi'i school)
    *   *Application:* Detailed rulings on killing geckos, snakes in homes, and the warning protocols for domestic snakes.

---

## 2. Jurisprudential Manuals by School

### Hanafi School
*   **Al-Bināyah Sharh al-Hidāyah** — *Badr al-Din al-Ayni*
*   **Radd al-Muhtār 'alā al-Durr al-Mukhtār** — *Ibn Abidin*
    *   *Application:* Hanafi methodology of *qiyās* (analogy) extending the permissibility of killing to any creature that causes actual harm, and their unique restriction on killing creatures that do not cause direct harm.

### Maliki School
*   **Al-Kāfī fī Fiqh Ahl al-Madīnah** — *Ibn Abd al-Barr*
*   **Sharh Mukhtasar Khalīl** — *Al-Kharashi*
    *   *Application:* Rulings on protected species, general permissions to remove harmful insects, and conditions of *Ihsan*.

### Shafi'i School
*   **Al-Majmū' Sharh al-Muhadhdhab** — *Imam al-Nawawi*
*   **Mughnī al-Muhtāj** — *Al-Khatib al-Shirbini*
    *   *Application:* Categorization of harmful vs. harmless creatures, and limits on killing ants, bees, and frogs.

### Hanbali School
*   **Al-Mughnī** — *Ibn Qudamah*
    *   *Application:* Exhaustive comparisons of rulings on Fawasiq, the legal status of stray dogs, and detailed exceptions for venomous creatures.

## 3. General Principles of Harm (Qawā'id Fiqhiyyah)

All secondary rulings are guided by the major legal maxims of Islamic law:
1.  **"Lā dharar wa lā dhirār"** (There should be neither harming nor reciprocating harm).
2.  **"Al-Dharar yuzāl"** (Harm must be eliminated).
3.  **"Al-Dharūrat tubīh al-mahdhūrāt"** (Necessities make permissible what is prohibited).
