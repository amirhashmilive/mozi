Islamic Jurisprudence

The Fiqh of Fawāsiq

Islamic animal rulings operate on a baseline of sanctuary and protection for all living things. However, specific exceptions are carved out for creatures whose natural behavior causes persistent harm to humans.

These are categorized as *Al-Mu'dhiyāt* or *Fawāsiq*. Rulings on these creatures represent a perfect integration of public safety and environmental proportionality.

The 5 Explicit Fawāsiq

These creatures are permitted to be culled even during the sacred pilgrimage (Ihram):

  • 🦂 Scorpion (Aqrab) — Venomous sting
  • 🐍 Snake (Hayyah) — Lethal venom
  • 🐀 Rat (Fa'rah) — Plague, fires, food loss
  • 🐕 Biting Dog (Kalb al-Aqur) — Rabies, aggression
  • 🦅 Kite / Crow — Vectors of disease
